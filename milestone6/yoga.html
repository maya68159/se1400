Resources

YogaRenew

This has been an excelent source for me and actually the program I am working on to get my yoga teacher certification. They have so many competent and capable instructors that teach so well. Despite the challenges of it being an online course, I don't feel that there is a lack of proper instruction at all; which was totally a worry of mine. This 200 hour course digs deep into the history, philosophy, anatomy, and of course asanas of yoga. If an in person class is not available to you, this is an excellent option.


Eastern Body Western Mind by Anodea Judith

This book has been so helpful for incorporating yogic principles into my daily life. It can be difficult to find time to be calm and still in the hustling life that is western culture. I think this book gives great insight on how to tend to the more spiriutal sides of ourselves yet still embrace the crazy of this life. Its an excellent read if that sounds like something you would like to learn more about.


Teaching Yoga, Essential Foundations and Techniques by Mark Stephens

This is an awesome book that helps those interseted in teaching yoga some of the basics. It bring your focus back to what matters and what to focus on so you dont get out of alignment with what really matters. It goes into deteail on how to go about creating a flow for a class. Not everyone may be intrerested in the idea of teaching a class someday, but for those who are, this is an awesome read.


The Art and Buisness of Teaching Yoga by Amy Ippoliti & Taro Smith

This is another read for those interested in teaching their own yoga classes. It is an excellent and comprehensive layout for how to go about becoming a yoga teacher and everything that comes with that in our modern world. It can really help navigate a lot of the technical side of yoga teaching. The pairing of this book and the Teaching Essentials book is an excellent starting plate for those wanting to start teaching and sharing their love for yoga in classes they lead.
